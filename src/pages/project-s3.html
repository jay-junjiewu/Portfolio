5 Band Audio Equaliser

I built a 5-band audio equalizer project using MATLAB, allowing real-time digital signal processing of audio frequencies. The equalizer is designed to provide users with the ability to manipulate the gain of five distinct frequency bands, offering a customisable audio experience.

Filter Design

The equalizer uses digital bandpass filters that are logarithmically spaced to align with the sensitivity of human hearing, ensuring a natural and intuitive audio adjustment experience. These filters were designed and implemented using MATLAB's DSP capabilities, allowing precise tuning of filter coefficients to effectively isolate and process specific frequency bands for optimal sound quality and performance.

Operating Principle

The gain adjustment is controlled via sliders that dynamically update the filter parameters, directly influencing the time-domain signal. These adjustments span a wide range of gain levels, from subtle to significant, which can be observed in the time graph. To ensure smooth processing, a sliding window buffer is used to handle real-time audio data, continuously updating the signal as the sliders are adjusted. This buffer allows for efficient management of the audio stream, ensuring that the filter coefficients are recalculated in real-time. Updates to the frequency response and spectrogram occur in real-time, with minimal latency, providing seamless transitions and a smooth audio experience as the frequency spectrum is adjusted.
